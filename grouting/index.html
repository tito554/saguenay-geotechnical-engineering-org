Grouting Design in Saguenay: Adapting Injection Strategies to the Saguenay Graben

Together, we solve the challenges of tomorrow.

LEARN MORE →

The Saguenay region sits atop a geological structure known as the Saguenay Graben, a rift valley where sedimentary rocks are overlain by thick sequences of marine clays and glacial tills. In our experience, the La Baie sector in particular shows extensive deposits of sensitive clay that can lose strength dramatically when disturbed, a challenge that directly shapes how we approach grouting design. Many projects require us to develop injection plans that can navigate the transition from dense till to these softer marine deposits — often within the same borehole. For sites near the Rivière Saguenay, we frequently integrate our grouting design with in-situ permeability testing to quantify how water flows through fractured bedrock, ensuring the injection parameters match the actual hydraulic conductivity of the formation.

In Saguenay's sensitive clays, a poorly designed injection program can do more harm than good — we focus on controlled, low-pressure permeation to avoid remolding the soil structure.

Methodology and scope

The freeze-thaw cycles that define Saguenay's winters — the city regularly sees temperatures dropping below -30°C — create a unique set of conditions for grouting operations. When we design injection programs for work that extends into late autumn, we must account for the thermal behavior of cement-based grouts and the potential for ice lens formation in the upper soil layers, which can alter flow paths and reduce penetration. This contrasts sharply with summer conditions when the active layer is fully thawed and grout takes are more predictable. Our approach typically couples low-mobility grouting for void filling in the granular overburden with permeation grouting in the fractured Precambrian basement where tighter joints require finer materials. For sites where excavation support is part of the scope, our grouting design often works in tandem with anchors to create a cohesive stabilization system that addresses both groundwater control and lateral earth pressures in the sensitive clay formations.

Local considerations

The risk profile for grouting in Saguenay shifts notably between the Chicoutimi and Jonquière boroughs. In Jonquière, where much of the urban development sits on a terrace of glaciofluvial sands and gravels, the primary concerns are uncontrolled grout travel beyond the target zone and the potential for differential heave if injection pressures are not tightly regulated. Over in the La Baie area, the deeper marine clay deposits introduce the danger of quick-clay landslides — a phenomenon tragically demonstrated by the 1971 Saint-Jean-Vianney slide just a few kilometers away. Here, even a minor pressure surge during injection can trigger progressive failure in the sensitive clay structure. Our design protocols for these zones incorporate conservative pressure limits and a staged injection sequence, verified by continuous heave monitoring, to ensure the ground improvement objective is met without compromising the stability of the adjacent formation.

Frequently asked questions

How do Saguenay's sensitive clays affect the choice of grouting method?

The sensitive clays found in the La Baie and lower Chicoutimi areas lose significant shear strength when remolded, so high-pressure or high-velocity injection can be counterproductive. We typically specify low-pressure permeation grouting with microfine cements or chemical grouts that can penetrate the soil matrix without causing hydrofracture or disturbance. The design always includes a pre-injection assessment of sensitivity and salinity, since the latter influences the effectiveness of certain chemical grouts.

What is the typical cost range for a grouting design project in Saguenay?

For a site-specific grouting design package — including geotechnical review, injection parameter specification, and construction-phase monitoring protocols — costs in the Saguenay area generally range from CA$1,560 to CA$6,090, depending on the project scale, number of injection zones, and the level of verification testing required. Larger curtain grouting campaigns or underpinning projects with extensive instrumentation will fall toward the upper end.

Can grouting be performed during Saguenay's winter months?

Yes, but it requires specific adaptations to the design and execution. Temperatures in Saguenay regularly drop below -30°C between December and March, which can affect grout set time and rheology. Our winter designs include heated batch plants, accelerated mix formulations, and thermal protection for injection lines. We also account for the presence of frozen ground in the upper 1.5 to 2.0 meters, which can act as a confining layer and alter the pressure distribution during injection.

How do you verify that the grouting has achieved the design intent?

Verification is built into the design from the start and depends on the project objective. For permeability reduction, we specify post-grouting packer tests (Lugeon tests) and compare results against pre-grouting baseline values. For underpinning or compensation grouting, we track real-time heave data from precision level surveys and in-place inclinometers, with trigger values defined in the design. In critical cases, we may also require post-grouting coring or cross-hole seismic tomography to confirm grout distribution in the target zone.
